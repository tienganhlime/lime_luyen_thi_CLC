Is Technology Making Us Less Focused?

Many students today rely on digital tools like smartphones, tablets, and laptops to study. These devices offer access to helpful apps, online libraries, and educational videos. However, some teachers worry that technology is making students less focused in class.

Studies have shown that students who constantly switch between apps or check their phones during lessons may struggle to complete tasks that need deep thinking. Even short notifications can break concentration, making it harder for students to return to serious work.

Still, others argue that it depends on how students use technology. A student using a tablet to organize notes or learn a new skill may perform better than one who uses it mostly for entertainment. Instead of removing technology, schools might need to teach students how to manage it wisely.
